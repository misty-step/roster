# Consulting Positioning

Showcase assets should make a prospect infer: this operator can turn ambiguous
AI workflows into reliable, inspectable software.

## Positioning Axes

Pick one primary axis per asset:

- **Operational trust**: visibility, auditability, safety, recovery.
- **Workflow leverage**: fewer handoffs, faster cycle, clearer ownership.
- **Technical taste**: deep modules, small surfaces, durable gates.
- **Executive clarity**: complex system made legible enough to buy or sponsor.
- **Artifact quality**: the demo, docs, and product feel finished.

Do not try to sell all axes equally. A focused case study beats a generic
capabilities page.

## Consulting Fit Questions

- What client pain does this prove you can handle?
- What decision-maker would understand the value in 30 seconds?
- What technical buyer would believe it after inspection?
- Which artifact proves judgment, not just implementation?
- What would make this feel like toyware, and how do we remove that?

## Copy Rules

- Lead with the operator's problem, not the implementation stack.
- Name the concrete change in behavior: sees, stops, audits, ships, routes,
  verifies, reconciles, explains.
- Use "AI" only when it changes the buyer's decision.
- Keep private-agent or local-machine details out of the public story unless
  local-first is the point.
- Put engineering rigor in proof sections, not hero copy.

## Case Study Spine

1. Before: what was opaque, risky, slow, or untrusted.
2. Intervention: the smallest system that changed the loop.
3. Proof: live demo, evidence packet, gate, or user outcome.
4. Judgment: tradeoffs and what was deliberately not built.
5. Consulting hook: the class of client problem this maps to.
