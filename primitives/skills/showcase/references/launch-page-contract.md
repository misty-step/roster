# Launch Page Contract

A launch page is a proof surface, not a decoration layer. Build it only after
the product has a demo path or a credible replay artifact.

## Required Inputs

- Audience and offer.
- Product category in plain language.
- One primary demo scenario.
- Evidence map for public assertions.
- Screenshots or video frames from the real product.
- CTA: contact, demo request, repo, waitlist, download, or case-study read.

## Page Shape

Use the repo's frontend stack when there is one. If none exists, shape first.

- Hero: product/category name, concrete value, real product visual.
- Problem: the painful before-state.
- Demo: embedded video, GIF, screenshots, or interactive route.
- Proof: evidence artifacts, gates, dogfood, case study, or architecture note.
- Fit: who should care and who should not.
- CTA: one next action.

## Anti-Slop Gate

- No generic "supercharge your workflow" copy.
- No fake dashboard metrics unless the fixture explicitly labels them.
- No decorative product screenshots that hide the actual state.
- No "coming soon" feature mixed into current capability without a vision label.
- No internal agent-process language.
- No landing page before the product moment itself has been inspected.

## Verification

Run `/design` on the rendered page and `/qa` on the route. The pass report must
name viewport(s), screenshots, console/network status where relevant, and the
evidence map status.
