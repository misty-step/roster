# Groom Lane Card

Use this shape for each independent lens in `mega-groom.md`. Commission roles,
not files. Do not give investigators the lead's preferred roadmap or other lane
findings; synthesis happens after reports return.

```markdown
Role: <specific perspective, not "general reviewer">

Objective: Find the most consequential delta between the live project and
world-class <lens outcome>.

Scope:
- Read: <vision, board snapshot, relevant repo surfaces>
- Exercise: <commands, routes, history, external research>
- Boundary: read-only; one perspective; no board mutations

Questions:
- <2–5 questions unique to this repo and lens>

Falsifier: <what evidence would overturn this lens's likely conclusion>
Evidence scope: <bounded sources this lane owns>

Return exactly:
**<Report ID> — <Lens>**
Evidence: <files, commands, routes, URLs, artifacts>
Top findings: <stable finding IDs; ranked; impact + confidence>
World-class delta: <what excellent requires>
Backlog move: <candidate ID(s), or no-emission; group finding IDs explicitly>
Provenance: <lane brief path/hash, raw report path, dispatch/run receipt>

Receipt: cite enough evidence that the lead can verify every proposed move.
```

Good lenses fail differently. If two prompts ask the same questions with
different job titles, they are one perspective in costumes. Repo-composed
lenses derive from a named invariant, persona, incident, competitor, or product
tension and state a distinct falsifier plus evidence scope. Preserve the exact
brief, raw report, and dispatch/run receipt for synthesis and criticism.
